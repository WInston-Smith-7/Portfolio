Color theory is an important part of design. Not only making sure to use color in an appropriate context depending on the target audience but to also make content that accessible to colorblind people.

color definitions

  • Hue: The pure form of color without alterations
  • Saturation: The intensity and purity of a color.
  • Value: the relative lightness or darkness of an image.
  • Shades: A color altered by differing levels of black applied
  • Tones: A color altered by differing levels of grey applied.
  • Tints: A color altered by differing levels of white applied.
  • Primary Colors: Pure colors that cannot be created by mixing other colors. Red, Blue and Green.
  • Secondary Colors: Colors made by mixing two primary colors. Violet, Green, Orange.
  • Tertiary Colors: Colors made from mixing a primary color and a secondary color.
  • Triadic Colors: Three colors that are split evenly across the color wheel.
  • Complementary Colors: Colors that complement eachother by sitting on the opposite end of the color wheel
  • Split Colors: A color and two colors adjacent to the opposite end of the color wheel.
  • Analogous Colors: Colors next to eachother on the color wheel.
  • Monochromatic: A type of color scheme that is defined by a single hue with varying tints, shades, and tones.
  • Vibrancy: the intensity brightness and energy of a hue.
  • Warm Colors: Colors closer to red on the spectrum and more vibrant.
  • Cool Colors: Colors closer to violet on the color spectrum and less vibrant.
  • Color Scheme: A combination of all the colors used in a project.
  • Print: Physical media
  • Screen: Digital media.
  • CMYK: Cyan Magenta Yellow Key. The way physical/print media is given color.
  • RGB: Red Green Blue. The way digital/screen media is given color.
